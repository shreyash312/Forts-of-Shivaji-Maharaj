Shivneri Fort

                    Shivneri Fort is a 17th-century military fortification located near Junnar in Pune district in Maharashtra, India.
            It is the birthplace of Shivaji, the founder of Maratha Empire. 
            Address: Shivneri Foot Path, Pune district, Junnar, Maharashtra 410502
            Phone: 084464 33350
            Owner: Government of India

                             Shivneri is the birthplace of the great king of Maratha empire – The Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Bhosale.
            Shivaji Maharaj was born on this fort in 1630 AD. This is the place where Shivaji Maharaj spent his most of the childhood.
            At this fort, he learned the qualities of great king and tactics required to build the empire. He was greatly influenced by
            the teachings of his mother-Jijabai. Shivneri has become a sacred place by the presence of Shivaji Maharaj.
            The fort is located at 3 kms from Junnar.

Geography of fort

            Altitude of a shivneri fort is 1067m (about 3500 feets) above Mean Sea Level and is located amidst the great Sahyadri Mountain Range.
            The shape of the fort is triangular & tapers to the one side. The fort is wide at the south end and narrow at the north side.The Northern
            side of the fort is called as Kadelot.
            The fort is surrounded by other forts like Harishchandragad, Narayangad, Hadsar, Bhairavgad, Jumnar, Chavand Jivadhan andother on its all sides.
            A map showing the location of almost all small and large forts of Maharashtra has been displayed at the Shivneri.

Things to see

           
            1) There are lots of worth watching places on Shivneri. The Shivneri has total 7 doors Maha darwaja, Peer darwaja,Phatak darwaja, Parvangicha darwaja,
                   Hatti darwaja, Kulambkat darwaja and Shipai darwaja.
            2) Birth-Home: The home where Shivaji Maharaj was born has been recently re-established.
            3) Statues: Statues Jijabaai and Little Shivaji at the south end of the fort.
            4) Shivai Temple: There is a temple of Shree Shivai Devi at the fort. The name of Shivaji Maharaj was kept after Shivai Devi Goddess.
            5) Badami Talao(Lake): Lake called Badami Talav is situated at the north side of the fort.                  
            6) Ancient Caves: Fort also has some underground Buddhist caves dating back to the first three centuries AD.
            7) It gives an amazing feeling of proud that you are at the place where one of India’s greatest kings was born.
            8) Water Reservoirs: The fort has several rock-cut water tanks. Ganga and Yamuna are large water tanks among them.
            9) Mughal Mosque: A mosque of the Mughal period is also present on the fort.

Best Time to visit

           
            Best Time to Visit: August to February is the ideal time to visit this fort.
            The environment remains pleasant & greener during this time period.
